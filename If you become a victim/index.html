A Quick Overview:

Becoming a victim of cybercrime can be distressing, but taking immediate action is crucial to minimize damage and protect yourself. Here's a simple guide on what to do if you're targeted by a cyber attack:


After Becoming a Victim: Steps to Take

1. Stay Calm and Act

Don't panic. Contact authorities and follow these steps:

  • Disconnect your devices from the internet.
  • Change passwords for compromised accounts and Enable two-factor authentication.
    Click here to download two-factor authentication app
  • Report the incident to law enforcement.
    If you do not know where to report, Click here to report the incident or visit IC3.gov

2. Secure Your Personal Information

Protect your sensitive data:

  • Contact your bank if financial information is involved. You may use this page to find your bank contact information here.
  • Regularly monitor your accounts for unusual activity.
  • Update your device's security software.

3. Report and Document

Keep a record of the incident:

  • Report the cybercrime to your local authorities.
    If you do not know where to report, Click here to report the incident.
  • Document emails, messages, or any evidence.
  • Contact your internet service provider (ISP) if needed.

4. Seek Professional Help

Consider expert assistance:

  • Contact a cybersecurity professional for advice.
    You may also contact us to assist you by visiting our website.
  • You may also use our Documentation page to mitigate the impact. Click here to visit.
  • Consult legal experts if necessary.

5. Learn and Prevent

Stay informed and safeguard yourself:

  • Understand how the attack happened.
  • Learn about cybersecurity best practices. There are many online resources available online for free.
    You may also find it on our Learning page.
  • Be cautious of suspicious emails, links, and calls.

Note

Cybercrime is a real threat that can affect anyone, regardless of their level of tech expertise. It's essential to be cautious and proactive in protecting your personal information and online presence. Remember that cybercriminals use various tactics to exploit vulnerabilities and trick unsuspecting individuals.

Danger: Think Before You Click

Be skeptical of unsolicited emails, messages, or offers that seem too good to be true. Always verify the source before sharing personal information or clicking on links.
