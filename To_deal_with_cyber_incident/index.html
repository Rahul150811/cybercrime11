Dealing with Live Cyber Incidents:

Facing a live cyber incident can be overwhelming, but taking swift and decisive action is crucial to mitigate potential damage. Here's a step-by-step guide on what to do if you're facing a live cyber incident:


Immediate Response: Stay Calm and Act Quickly

1. Isolate and Disconnect

If you suspect a live cyber attack, take these steps:

  • Isolate the affected device or network segment from the rest of your network to prevent further spread.
  • Disconnect the affected devices from the internet to minimize communication with the attacker.
  • If applicable, power off or disconnect compromised devices to prevent ongoing damage.

2. Assess and Identify

Evaluate the situation and determine the nature of the attack:

  • Identify the type of attack (e.g., phishing, malware) and its scope.
  • Take notes of any unusual behavior or messages on your device.

3. Contact Support

Reach out for assistance:

  • Contact your organization's IT department or a trusted technical friend for help.
  • If you suspect a financial or personal data breach, get in touch with your bank or credit card provider.

4. Preserve Evidence

Document the incident:

  • Take screenshots or photos of any suspicious activity.
  • Save copies of any unusual messages or files for later analysis.

5. Monitor and Recover

Stay vigilant and restore your security:

  • Regularly check your accounts for any unauthorized transactions or changes.
  • Restore your devices from clean backups to ensure no malicious software remains.
  • Update your passwords and enable two-factor authentication for added security.

Note

Dealing with a live cyber incident can be stressful, but taking immediate action and seeking help can greatly minimize potential harm. Remember, staying calm and informed is key to protecting yourself online.

Stay Vigilant and Prepared

Cyber incidents can happen to anyone, but by following these steps and staying cautious, you can take control of the situation and protect your digital life.
