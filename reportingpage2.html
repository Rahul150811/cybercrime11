What is a Phishing attack?


Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers use deceptive tactics to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as login credentials, financial data, or personal details. This is often done through fake emails, websites, or messages that appear to be from trusted sources.

Identifying Phishing: Signs to Watch For


Email Phishing: Scam Email Signs

  • The email contains urgent language, pressuring you to take immediate action.
  • Poor spelling and grammar in the email, indicating it might be fraudulent.
  • Suspicious email address or a mismatched sender name (e.g., email claims to be from your bank but has an unrelated address).
  • Requests for sensitive information like passwords, Social Security numbers, or financial details via email.

Spear Phishing: Scam Email Signs

  • The email is personalized with your name or references to your workplace, but it's unexpected or unusual.
  • Unusual requests from colleagues or superiors, especially involving money or confidential data.
  • The sender's email address looks similar to a known contact but has slight variations.

Whaling Attacks: Signs of Impersonation

  • You receive an email from a high-ranking executive requesting sensitive information without prior notice or proper context.
  • The email emphasizes confidentiality and insists on bypassing regular security procedures.

Pharming: Signs of Redirection

  • The website's URL looks correct, but the page asks for login credentials or sensitive data unexpectedly.
  • The website's design and layout seem slightly off compared to the legitimate site.

Smishing: Signs of Suspicious Text Messages

  • The text message asks you to click on a link or call a number urgently.
  • The message comes from an unknown or unexpected sender.
  • The content of the text seems too good to be true (e.g., winning a prize you didn't enter).

Vishing: Signs of Suspicious Calls

  • The caller claims to be from a government agency or well-known organization but asks for personal information.
  • The caller uses scare tactics, creating a sense of urgency or fear to make you reveal sensitive data.
