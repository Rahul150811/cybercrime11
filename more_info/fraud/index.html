Fraudulent activities aim to deceive individuals or organizations for financial gain, personal information, or other benefits. Being vigilant and informed can help you stay safe from fraudsters. Here's what you need to know:

1. Recognize Common Fraud Schemes

Familiarize yourself with common fraud tactics:

  • Phishing: Unsolicited emails, messages, or calls that trick you into revealing personal or financial information.
  • Impersonation: Scammers pretending to be legitimate organizations, friends, or family to gain your trust.
  • Online Shopping Scams: Fake websites, counterfeit products, or sellers who don't deliver after receiving payment.
  • Investment Scams: Promises of high returns with little risk. Always research before investing.
  • Romance Scams: Fake online relationships used to extract money or personal information.

2. Protect Your Personal Information

Guard your sensitive data:

  • Never share personal or financial information through email or unfamiliar websites.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your online accounts and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Be cautious when sharing information on social media; fraudsters can use it to impersonate you.

3. Verify Requests and Offers

Stay skeptical and verify before taking action:

  • If you receive an unexpected request for money or information, independently verify it with known contact information.
  • Be cautious of offers that seem too good to be true; scammers often use this tactic to lure victims.

4. Secure Your Devices

Protect your devices from cyber threats:

  • Keep your operating system, software, and security programs up to date.
  • Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware software to detect and prevent malicious activities.

5. Report Fraudulent Activities

If you encounter or suspect fraud:

  • Report the incident to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) online or by phone.
  • Contact your local law enforcement if you've been a victim of financial fraud.

Stay Informed

Fraud can take many forms, and scammers are always coming up with new tactics. Staying informed and cautious is your best defense against falling victim to fraudulent activities.


For more information and guidance, you can visit the FTC Scam Alerts page.
