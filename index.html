Reporting Solutions for Every Cyber Threat

Report Cyber Threats

Phishing

Scammers try to trick you into giving away sensitive information, like passwords or credit card details, through deceptive emails or fake websites. Report suspicious messages or sites to stay safe online.

Report

Ransomware

This form of cybercrime, employs malicious software to lock your computer or files, demanding a ransom for release. If affected, report the incident promptly to prevent further harm and seek assistance.

Report

Online Harassment - Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is when people use social media to hurt, scare, or bully others. If you see or experience mean messages or rumors online, report them to help make the internet safer.

Report

Tampering

Unauthorized changes made to software, hardware, or data with the intention of causing harm or disruption. If you suspect or come across evidence of tampering, it is crucial to report it to the appropriate authorities.

Report

Identity Theft

Has someone unlawfully obtained and used your personal information for fraudulent activities or scams? Prevent further harm by promptly reporting any cases of identity theft. Protect your identity from misuse and stay vigilant against potential threats.

Report

Social Media Hacked

Social media hacking can lead to misuse of your personal info or unauthorized posts. Report any instances of unauthorized access to regain control and protect your privacy. Act promptly to safeguard your accounts and personal data.

Report

Credit Card Fraud/Stolen

If you suspect credit card information theft and unauthorized transactions, report it immediately. We'll assist you in preventing further fraud and safeguarding your financial well-being. Act promptly to secure your accounts and finances.

Report

Computer/Phone Hacked

Device hacking involves unauthorized access to your computer or electronic device using malicious software like viruses. If you suspect hacking, seek cybersecurity experts' help to mitigate damage and prevent further access. Act quickly to protect your privacy and data from potential threats.

Report

Intellectual Property Theft

Intellectual property theft includes unauthorized use, reproduction, or distribution of creative works like patents or copyrighted materials. Report such instances to protect the rights of creators and innovators.

Report

Scam Phone Call/Message

Scammers use deceptive phone calls to extort money or obtain personal information. Report suspicious calls to take action against these scammers and protect others from falling victim to their tricks. Stay alert and report any suspicious activity.

How to Report a Cybercrime

If you've been a victim of online crime, don't lose hope. Visit the Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) at www.IC3.gov. By reporting your crime, you help the FBI track down cyber criminals worldwide. Provide detailed information about the incident, including dates, times, and relevant details. Your report contributes to identifying patterns, gathering evidence, and bringing criminals to justice. Reporting to the IC3 not only helps your case but also protects others from similar crimes. The IC3 also provides resources and tips to stay vigilant against online threats. Join the fight against cybercrime by reporting to the IC3 today.

Answers to Your Most Common Questions

Read Our FAQ's

Q: What should I do if I think I have been a victim of a cybercrime?

A: If you suspect you have been a victim of a cybercrime, take immediate action. Secure your devices, change passwords, and disconnect from the internet. Report the incident to our website or your local law enforcement agency, and consider seeking assistance from cybersecurity professionals.

Q: How can I protect myself from cyber threats and stay safe online?

A: Protecting yourself from cyber threats starts with practicing good cybersecurity hygiene. Use strong and unique passwords, keep your devices and software up to date, be cautious of suspicious emails or links, and regularly backup your important data. Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity best practices through our educational resources.

Q: What information should I include when reporting a cybercrime?

A: When reporting a cybercrime, include as much information as possible, such as the nature of the incident, any evidence you have, the date and time it occurred, and any relevant details about the perpetrator or suspicious activities. The more information you provide, the better equipped law enforcement or cybersecurity experts will be to investigate the incident.

Q: Can I report cybercrimes anonymously?

A: Yes, we understand the need for anonymity in certain situations. You have the option to submit a report anonymously on our website. However, providing contact information can be helpful for follow-up questions or updates on your reported incident. Rest assured that we treat all information with strict confidentiality.

Q: How can I contribute to improving cybersecurity efforts?

A: Besides reporting cybercrimes, you can raise awareness about cybersecurity among your friends, family, and community. Share our resources, educate others about common threats, and encourage them to adopt secure practices.
