Risk Assessment

This cyber risk assessment is a simple and easy-to-use tool that helps you assess your risk of being hacked or becoming a victim of other cybercrimes. You will need to answer a series of questions about your internet habits and security practices. Based on your answers, the tool will generate a score and provide you with personalized recommendations for how to improve your internet hygiene.

1. Password Security:

  • Do you use strong and unique passwords for your online accounts?
  • How often do you change your passwords?
  • Do you use a password manager to store and generate complex passwords?

2. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

  • Do you enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible?
  • Are you aware of the types of MFA available (e.g., SMS codes, authenticator apps, hardware tokens)?

3. Device Security:

  • Do you keep your computer, smartphone, and other devices up to date with the latest security patches and updates?
  • Do you use security software (e.g., antivirus) on your devices?
  • Is your smartphone protected with a PIN, password, or biometric authentication?

4. Social Engineering Awareness:

  • Are you cautious about sharing personal information online or over the phone?
  • Do you know how to recognize phishing emails and scam calls?

5. Data Backup:

  • Do you regularly back up your important data and files?
  • How often do you test your backups to ensure they can be restored?

6. Software and App Downloads:

  • Do you only download software and apps from reputable sources (e.g., official app stores)?
  • Are you cautious about granting permissions to apps on your devices?

7. Social Media Privacy:

  • Have you reviewed and adjusted your privacy settings on social media platforms?
  • Do you limit the amount of personal information you share publicly on social media?

8. Do you visit unknown websites

  • Do you download or watch movies on unknown websites?
  • Do you know it is safe to visit unknown websites?
