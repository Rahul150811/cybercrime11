How to Analyse Phishing and Scam Emails

Identifying Red Flags🚩

  • Email

Flag 1: Sender's Email Address

Check the sender's email address. Phishing emails often come from addresses that may look similar to legitimate ones but have subtle misspellings or variations.

Flag 2: Urgency and Threats

Phishing emails create a sense of urgency and may include threats to prompt immediate action. Legitimate organizations usually don't threaten or pressure users.

Flag 3: Unsolicited Nature

If you didn't expect the email or haven't signed up for related services, it's likely a phishing attempt.

Flag 4: Unusual Content

Be cautious of unexpected or unusual content, especially those claiming you've won a prize, inherited money, or are in urgent need of assistance.

Flag 5: Links and Hovering

Hover over links to reveal the actual destination. Phishing emails often include links to fake websites designed to collect personal information.

Flag 6: Email Content

Poor grammar, spelling mistakes, or awkward language can indicate a phishing attempt. Legitimate organizations typically proofread their communications.

Flag 7: Requests for Personal Information

Legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive information (passwords, credit card details) via email.

Flag 8: Unusual Attachments

Avoid opening attachments in unsolicited emails. Malicious attachments can contain malware.
