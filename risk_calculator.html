Why Use the Risk Assessment Calculator?

In today's world, it's essential to protect your assets, whether they are personal belongings or valuable equipment for your business. However, implementing security measures can come at a cost. This calculator empowers you to assess the financial implications of security decisions and determine whether the investment is justified.

How does it Work: Explanation of Terms

  • Asset Value ($):

    The total financial value of the asset(s) you want to assess.

    Example: If you are assessing five laptops, and each laptop is valued at $1,000, the total asset value would be $5,000.

  • Exposure Factor (EF):

    A measure of how vulnerable your assets are to a potential risk or threat. It represents the portion of an asset's value that could be lost in a security incident.

    Example: If you estimate that 30% of the value of your laptops could be lost in a theft (EF: 0.3), then the exposure factor is 0.3.

  • Threat Frequency (per year):

    An estimate of how often you expect security incidents or threats to occur in a given year.

    Example: If you expect that, on average, there might 10 theft incidents involving laptops in your area per year, the threat frequency would be 10 per year.

  • Single Loss Expectancy (SLE):

    The expected financial loss that would result from a single security incident or threat.

    Formula: SLE = Asset Value ($) * Exposure Factor (EF)

    Example: With an asset value of $5,000 and an exposure factor of 0.3, the SLE for a single laptop theft incident would be $1,500.

  • Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE):

    The expected annual financial loss due to security incidents or threats. It accounts for the likelihood of multiple incidents occurring over a year.

    Formula: ALE = SLE * Threat Frequency (per year)

    Example: If you expect 10 laptop theft incidents per year and the SLE for a single incident is $1,500, the ALE would be $15,000 per year.

  • Cost of Security Measure ($):

    The expense associated with implementing a security measure to mitigate or prevent security incidents.

    Example: If you plan to invest $500 in laptop cable locks as a security measure, the cost of the security measure would be $500.

Make informed security decisions

With the Risk Assessment Calculator, you can make informed decisions about protecting your assets. Whether you're a small business owner considering security investments or an individual safeguarding personal belongings, our tool will help you evaluate risks and costs, enabling you to take the right steps toward security and peace of mind.
